1. Network Diagnostic Tests (NDT7) & M-Lab

⚠️ Critical Notice Regarding Speed Test Data This Application runs network performance tests (Speed Tests) using the NDT7 protocol maintained by Measurement Lab (M-Lab).

When you run a speed test in this Application, the test is conducted between your device and an M-Lab server. M-Lab collects specific data to promote scientific research on Internet performance.

What is collected by M-Lab?

  • IP Address: The public-facing Internet Protocol (IP) address of your device is collected to understand network topology.
  • Performance Data: Speed, latency, jitter, and other network diagnostic metrics.

Is this data private?

No. To support open Internet research, M-Lab publishes all test data publicly under a Creative Commons license. By running a test, you acknowledge that your IP address and test results will be included in M-Lab's public dataset.

GDPR/Data Deletion Exception: Because M-Lab is a public scientific archive, test results (including IP addresses) are permanent. Once a test is completed, we cannot delete the data from M-Lab's dataset, even if you revoke your consent later. Revoking consent in the app will only prevent future tests from being shared.

For more details, review the M-Lab Privacy Policy.

3. GDPR Rights (European Union & UK)

If you are located in the EEA or UK, you have specific rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right to Access: You may request copies of your personal data.
  • Right to Rectification: You may request that we correct any information you believe is inaccurate.
  • Right to Erasure: You may request that we erase your personal data, under certain conditions. Note: This does not apply to M-Lab public data as it is archived for scientific research.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: You can withdraw your consent for personalized ads at any time via the app settings.

To exercise these rights, please contact us at the email provided below.

4. California Privacy Rights (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you have the right to:
